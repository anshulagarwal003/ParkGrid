ParkGrid

A parking discovery layer that helps drivers find the closest meter now, and in v2 compares nearby marketplace inventory to surface the best price fast.

Boston parking search • route optimization • marketplace referrals

Problem

⏱️

Parking search is wasted time

Drivers still bounce between streets, meters, and apps when they need a spot immediately near a destination.

🧩

Pricing is fragmented

Street meters, garages, and parking apps expose different inventory and prices in separate interfaces.

📱

Users want one decision surface

The best experience is not another map alone; it is the fastest route to the cheapest acceptable spot nearby.

Current product

What ParkGrid already does

The repo is a Streamlit app that reads Boston parking meter data, computes distance with a Haversine function, filters by user-selected radius, and returns the nearest parking options with rate, hours, max time, slot count, and Google Maps navigation.

📍

Location-based search

Lat/lon input with a 0.25 km to 5 km search radius.

🗺️

Maps routing

Single-stop and multi-stop Google Maps directions links.

Business model for v2

Marketplace layer

Compare meter + app inventory

Version 2 expands beyond meter data to integrate separate datasets for accessible parking, EV parking, and parking rules, then compares marketplace inventory to rank nearby options by distance, price, and constraints so the user gets the optimal parking price with less search friction.

  • • ParkGrid becomes the decision engine, not just a map.
  • • Users see the best price near destination in one interface.
  • • Routing still happens in the destination app or Google Maps.

Monetization

Referral and affiliate revenue

Monetization is simple: ParkGrid sends high-intent users to booking partners and earns referral or affiliate revenue when a parking reservation converts.
